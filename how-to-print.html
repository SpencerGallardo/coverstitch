How to Print Game & Movie Covers

Find the right art, stitch it together with CoverStitch, and print professional-looking covers at home. This guide covers everything from sourcing to cutting.

Step 1 — Where to Find Cover Art

High-resolution cover art is the foundation. Blurry source images will produce blurry prints no matter how good your printer is. Here are the best sources:

  • The Cover Project Best for game cases
  • MobyGames Huge game database, scanned covers
  • Laser Disc Database Classic film covers
  • Fanart.tv High-res movie & TV covers
  • LaunchBox Games Database Large game art library, community-sourced
Pro Tip Always look for images at 300 DPI or larger. For PS2 covers, the full wrap at 300 DPI is about 3235×2173px. If you can only find smaller images, scale them up with an AI upscaler like Upscayl or Topaz before stitching.

Scanning your own covers

If you own the original case and want a perfect digital copy, scanning is the gold standard. Use a flatbed scanner at 600 DPI, scan front and back separately, then crop and clean up in an image editor. Import both panels into CoverStitch alongside any spine art.

Step 3 — Printing Tips

Paper selection

Photo paper (glossy or satin) is the best choice. Matte photo paper works if you prefer a less reflective finish. Plain printer paper will look washed out and won't hold up inside the case. Use 200–250 gsm weight for a feel close to original inserts.

Print at 100% scale — no scaling

This is the most common mistake. When you open the image in your photo viewer or print dialog, make sure "Scale to fit" or "Fit to page" is turned OFF. Print at exactly 100% / actual size. CoverStitch already outputs the image at the exact physical dimensions for the selected preset at 300 DPI.

Recommended print settings Paper size: A4 or Letter (depending on case — see dimensions guide). Orientation: Landscape. Scale: 100% / Actual Size. Quality: Best / Photo. Colour profile: sRGB. Borderless printing: On if available.

Cutting

A paper trimmer (guillotine cutter) gives clean, straight edges. Scissors work but are harder to keep straight over a long cut. Mark your fold lines lightly with a bone folder before folding the spine — this prevents cracking on glossy paper.

Laminating (optional)

Running the printed cover through a laminator adds durability and gives it a finish very close to a commercial insert. Use 80-micron pouches for a flexible result — thicker pouches can make the cover too stiff to fit back in the case. Always trim after laminating, not before.

✓

Step 4 — Choosing the Right Case Preset

Each case format has different physical dimensions. Getting these right means your print will fold at exactly the right point and fit perfectly inside the case. CoverStitch has built-in presets for all major formats:

  • PS2 / DVD Game: 130×184mm front & back panels, 14mm spine. Full wrap is approximately 274×184mm.
  • PS5: 128×148mm front & back panels, 14mm spine. Slightly smaller height than PS2.
  • Nintendo Switch: 128×160mm front & back panels, 10mm spine. Narrower spine than PS2.
  • DVD: Same as PS2 — 130×184mm covers, 14mm spine.
  • Blu-ray: 128×148mm covers, 12mm spine.
  • Steelbook: 138×172mm covers, 14mm spine.

For a full breakdown of dimensions in mm and pixels, see the case dimensions reference.
